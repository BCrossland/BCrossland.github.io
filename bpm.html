In 2017 when BPM was founded, grime & drill were not part of the St. Andrews cultural landscape. That had to change. In a town that churned out rather repetitive and stale black tie events, founder Ahmed Shareefy brought a breath of fresh air.

When I joined the founding team the key hurdle was being top of mind in the social calendar. In a market saturated with events, we needed to stand out.
We knew that building relationships was key in nurturing a successful event. We quickly built ongoing partnerships with local venues, which allowed us to do more with custom sound systems and lighting. To cut out the laborious physical ticket collection we worked closely with FIXR to deliver a seamless digital ticket management and sales process.

Importantly we also kept our audience engaged post-event with curated Spotify playlists and branded items such as bottle openers & phone cases.

Over four years BPM became a mainstay event of St. Andrews nightlife. Tickets would frequently sell out in under 10 minutes and the event spaces were at capacity. BPM has since expand to London, hosting consistently packed events featuring prominent artists such as Sam Wise, ONEMAN & A2 while retaining those same venue and technology partners that we started with.
