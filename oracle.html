If you work in tech, you’ve probably heard of Oracle… maybe you’ve even found that we now provide cloud. What you may not know is that Oracle has a dedicated startup program; Oracle for Startups. In June 2021, I was brought into Oracle for Startups to assist in managing the ambassador & mentor network; a volunteer network of Oracle employees with a shared enthusiasm for startups and innovation, on hand to provide founders with support and advice.

Most cloud providers - ahem, AWS - shower a founder with free credits then leave them stranded on the back end, often leaving teams with a bloated infrastructure and no one to talk to. We wanted to change that. These are just some of the ways we provide a more human touch and in 2022 we decided to go even further; launching Oracle for Startups official partnerships.
Our goal was to empower our ambassadors find and nurture strategic partnerships with incubators, venture capital and private equity to create meaningful conversations with truly innovative founders.

We knew that this needed to be easy with a straight-forward approval process. So we set a wide funnel, allowing ambassadors to pursue their interests, trusting the ambassador to lead the conversation. Our core team would be on hand to prove support whenever needed; that includes the sourcing or promo codes, featured speakers and only the most hype merchandise.

In the short time that we have planned and launched Oracle for Startups partnerships, I am really proud of the results. Many partnerships have already been launched due to the outstanding diligence of our ambassadors and the speed of the internal approval process. We’ve successfully launched partnerships with government backed incubators across Latin America as well as such as globally attended events such as Enterprise Ireland.
